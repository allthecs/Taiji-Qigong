What is Taiji Qigong?

“Tai Chi” or "Taiji" means “Supreme Ultimate,” representing the “Cosmos” principles of yin and yang. Taiji is rooted in Qigong the Chinese practice of cultivating ‘Qi’ (Vital Energy) by aligning breath, movement, and awareness for healing.

Taiji (in all forms and spellings) can be traced back hundreds of years. Qigong in its’ many guises, can be traced back seven thousand years. That said, Chairman Mao Zedongs' Cultural Revolution (1949-1976), banned all forms of Taiji.  Under his regime anyone discovered practising Taiji put themselves and their family at risked of public ridicule and imprisonment.

Post Mao Zedong:

Today there are countless forms of Taiji Qigong (& Taiji ch'üan) taught around the world. Professor Lin Houshengs’ Taiji Qigong Shibashi is arguably the most widely taught/practiced Taiji Qigong for health and wellbeing by older peole world wide.

Taiji ch'üan (Taijiquan/shadow boxing) is also practiced world wide having been developed and popularised by Professor Cheng Man Ch’ing (1902-1975). Taiji Qigong & Taiji ch'üan share many characteristics and often taught together Taiji ch'üan is however primarily martial in practice.

Other forms of Taiji worth a mention include: Yang, Hao, Wu, Chen, and Sun. Regardless of origin/school all Tai Chi share at least one of four core elements: ‘Martial’, Spiritual, Physical and Mental (Wellbeing).

Professor Lin Housheng's Taiji Qigong Shibashi is based on the philosophy and principles of Qigong having choreographed movements and postures, Qigong breathing and modified ‘Yang Style’ Tai Chi.

 
Chines  whispers:

When searching the internet for Taiji Qigong tutorials, the first thing to jump out is the myriad  of different 'schools' of Taiji Qigong practice. Most use the same set of moves developed by Professor Lin Housheng, but with different 'interpretations' of the original chorography with the possible exception to the wiji, bow and horse stances.

Doing it the wrong way!

Except for one small caveat, there is no right or wrong way to practice Taiji Qigong Shibashi. The caveat being, if you find any move uncomfortable or stressful either a) moderate it, or b) avoid it.

When practiced the right way you will be able to effortlessly merge even-paced movements with rhythmic breathing.  You will also be aware of the sensations arising from the 'life energy' streaming throughout the body as you practice.

Benefits

Taiji Qigong Shibashi isn't just good for your health and wellbeing, it has the advantage of being able to be practiced by anyone anywhere at anytime. At home on your own or with friends, in a large group on the beach or a park.  It doesn’t even require specialist equipment or levels of ability or fitness. (Although, relaxing meditative or traditional Taiji Qigong music, is helpful) . So the only barrier to practising Taiji Qigong is the excuse you make for not having a go!

The many health benefits of regular Taiji Qigong are recognised and encouraged by western health services including the NHS.

The Five Elements

The 5  Element are an ancient understanding of the natural world expressed through the movement and transformation of the environment (Qi). The elements are interrelated and influence each other.  All life including Humans are an integral part of the system. The elements together with Yin and Yang, are the main methods used in Traditional Chinese Medicine to help inform our connection to and relationship with our environment and the universe. The elements traditional are: Metal, Water, Wood, Fire and Earth.

Metal.
 Associated with the lungs, colour white, sound ‘SSSS’ and the autumn.

Water.
Associated with the kidneys, colour: blue, sound ‘CHOOO’ and the winter.

Wood.
            Associated with the liver, colour: green, sound ‘SHHH’ and the spring.

Fire.
Associated with the heart colour: red, sound ‘HAAHHHH’  and the summer.

Earth.
Associated with the spleen, colour: yellow, sound: WHOOOO and late summer.

However, there are hundreds of interpretations or expressions (no exaggeration) of the 5 elements. Some (in my opinion) are perhaps a bit ‘made-de-up-pee’ lacking any direct cultural or historical link. 

There are also 5 animal elements to consider that can be traced back for more than 2 millennia i.e. tiger, deer, bear, monkey and crane.  Often couple with the animal elements are 'animal' martial art forms to numerous to cover here:

Qigong Meditation
Five Elements according to Simon Blow

[Simons’] meditation focuses on the five solid (Yin) organs of the body and their corresponding element:-  Heart (Fire), Spleen (Earth), Lungs (Metal), Kidneys (Water) and Liver (Wood). According to Simon Blow these organs function a bit like batteries and when we bring our awareness and energy to them, they help our Qi flow smoothly and bring harmony to mind, body and spirit. The Qi from these organs then supplies the flowing (Yang) organs : Small Intestine (Fire), Stomach (Earth), Large Intestine (Metal), Urinary Bladder (Water) and Gall Bladder (Wood).”

Simon Blow has a serious back catalogue  of work on meditation and Taiji qigong and is one of many authors  to publish on this subject.

Note: Element order changes depending on school of thought/academy.  Simon Blow suggests starting with Fire or Heart whilst others start with Metal or Lungs and others wood or liver.
